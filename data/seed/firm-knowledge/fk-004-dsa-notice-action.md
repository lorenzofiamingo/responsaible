For hosting providers in scope of the Digital Services Act, our standard notice-and-
action clause must give any individual or entity a mechanism to flag specific items of
allegedly illegal content (Article 16), and commit the provider to act in a timely,
diligent, non-arbitrary way. Where the provider removes or disables content, it must
issue a clear statement of reasons to the affected recipient (Article 17). Build in
priority handling for trusted flaggers and avoid drafting that promises a fixed removal
SLA — diligence is the standard, not a guaranteed timeframe.
